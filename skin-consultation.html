Medical history

This information is essential to ensure prescription skincare is safe for you. All responses are reviewed by our Independent Nurse Prescriber and held in strict medical confidence.

This includes prescription medications, supplements, herbal remedies, the contraceptive pill, HRT and any over-the-counter products.

Important: You must not commence any new skincare treatment, including prescription products, while you have an active skin infection. Please consult your GP for advice before proceeding. You may still complete this consultation and we will hold your assessment until you are clear.

Important — Pregnancy & Contraception
Tretinoin is a Category X teratogen and is strictly contraindicated in pregnancy. The following questions are a mandatory clinical requirement for any prescription involving tretinoin or retinoid-based products.

We are unable to prescribe tretinoin or retinoid-based products during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Please contact us to discuss alternative skincare options that are safe for use during pregnancy. Your consultation will be reviewed for non-retinoid alternatives.

I agree to use reliable contraception throughout any tretinoin treatment, perform a pregnancy test if my period is late, stop treatment and contact Sebastian Rose immediately if pregnancy is suspected, and use additional barrier protection if advised.

Your current skincare

Understanding your existing skincare routine helps us prescribe safely and avoid interactions with active ingredients.

Daily SPF 50 is mandatory when using prescription skincare including tretinoin and hydroquinone. These products significantly increase UV sensitivity — using them without adequate sun protection will worsen pigmentation and damage. If you do not currently use SPF 50, this must be in place before treatment begins. We can recommend and supply a suitable SPF alongside your prescription.

Treatment history

Certain prescription ingredients must be paused around professional treatments. Please answer honestly to ensure your safety.

Including laser, microneedling, microdermabrasion, chemical peels, IPL, radiofrequency or similar.

Certain prescription products — particularly tretinoin and hydroquinone — must be paused before professional skin treatments. Your prescriber will advise you on exactly when to pause and resume based on the specific treatment you are having.
